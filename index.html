Satchel Inventory

What Survived the Fire

Oilskin satchel, split along one seam. One field book swollen with damp. One marked hill sketch folded beneath a ration ledger. Three scraps pinned together with wire. Blood on the clasp. Soot pressed into every page.

The notes are not clean. At least three hands worked the hill, and a fourth, rougher hand added circles, slashes, and questions after the scouts were taken. Nothing here should be treated as settled fact.

Reader's Caution

What This Folio Tries to Do

These pages do not explain the hill. They only record what one doomed party thought they understood before going in. That is why distances are rough, the tone is tense, and some conclusions do not agree.

"If the road is watched, take the smoke. If the smoke is folly, we lose an hour and not our necks."
