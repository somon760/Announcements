First Watch

First Sight from the Vale

The tooth shows itself before noon: bare crown, dark woods, one hard road climbing and doubling back across the face.

Smoke rises somewhere below the shoulder, though the hill swallows the source when you try to mark it cleanly.

If the stone is hollow, it still breathes.

Trace: the phrase about the hill breathing matches the cramped speculative hand later tied to Ysella Quill.

Second Watch

On the Road Below the Tooth

The road is old work, too straight in the cut and too stubborn under the wash to be a goat track.

Fresh descent marks by morning light. Four sets, maybe five, all booted and all careless. Whoever uses it believes the hill keeps them safe.

If we watch from cover and keep no flame, we may learn whether they move by day or by dark.

Trace: the block hand and watch-minded phrasing align with Brother Caldus Rey and his watch slate.

Third Watch

Smoke Under the South Face

Found the reek before we found the crack. Warm draft. Soot on the lip. Rock stained from long use.

Edric says no grown soul in kit will fit. Tamsin marked otherwise: a lean climber might, if pack and steel go down by line after.

Tamsin swore someone upslope had seen us and would not move on until the brush settled again. Edric asked whether every pine knot was now an orc eye, and Ysella called it smoke and nerves. Brother Caldus laughed with them, though not for long.

Do not send anyone who fears tight stone.

Trace: the hooked T in the margin matches Tamsin Vale and the reconstructed scout insignia.

Third Watch, Later

Broken Brush on the East Scar

Brush on the east side is torn low and inward. Not wind. Not deer. There is old spoil there, half swallowed by roots.

We saw a darker cut behind the growth and lost the light before we could test it. The ground there keeps other tracks badly.

No one agreed on what made them.

Trace: the narrow shorthand around "spoil" and "lost the light" matches notes preserved with Ysella Quill's field papers.

Fourth Watch

The Black Water

East of the hill lies black water and sucking shore. Midges enough to strip patience from a saint.

Brother Caldus calls it dead ground: too open, too shallow, nothing there but rot and frogs. I marked it only so no one wastes daylight proving him right.

The place still carries sound strangely at dusk.

Fourth Watch, Late

The Crown

Two hard hours to the summit and blood from both palms on the last rise. Fine view. No prize.

No break in the stone, no old signal post, no sure vent. If there was ever something worth finding on the crown, it is gone now.

Do not spend strength there unless you need the lay of the forest.

Trace: the gouged pressure and torn-palm complaint fit Edric Thorne better than any other hand in the book.

Last Leaf

Moonset

We wait until the road draws the watch.

If the lower sentries keep their eyes on the front, Tamsin and Edric take line, hook, and the hooded lamp to the smoke crack. If Edric is right, we lose an hour. If Tamsin is right,

the rest of the line is blackened away

Trace: the final plan ties the smoke leaf back to Tamsin Vale and the hauling gear in Edric Thorne's bundle.

Loose Gloss

Different pressure in the final two entries. Same hand, less sleep.

Contradiction

One scout marks the smoke as folly. Another builds the whole plan around it.

Absence

No one writes what happened after moonset. No returning hand added to the book.

Hands Compared

Tamsin hooks the T, Caldus writes like a ledger, Ysella narrows every speculative note, and Edric presses hard enough to bruise the page.

The notebook ends before the scouts made their attempt.
