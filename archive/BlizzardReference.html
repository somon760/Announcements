Blizzard Crawl Mini-Game: Quick Reference for Players

Preparation Phase in Bryn Shander

  • You have one in-game day to prepare for the pursuit of the horde.
  • Focus your efforts on:
    • Gathering supplies.
    • Physical, mental, or magical preparations.
    • Scouting or gaining information to aid the pursuit.
  • Describe your actions in-character, individually or collaboratively.

Setup: Initial Party Conditions

  • Blizzard Tokens: Shared resource starting with a determined number.
  • Exhaustion Levels: Start with none unless otherwise noted.
  • Shelter Condition: Begin with a fully functional tent.

Environment & Pursuit Dynamics: Staying close to the horde offers relief from the worst of the storm but increases the risk of encounters. Traveling farther increases storm exposure but reduces direct confrontations.

Daily Phases

1. Morning Phase

  • Resolve Daily Environmental Toll: Lose 1 Blizzard Token daily due to storm conditions.

  • Camp Breakdown Challenge: DC 14 (Strength, Dexterity, Survival, or Wisdom). Results affect your starting position:

    • Success: Choose to move closer, stay at the same position, or move farther back.
    • Failure: Stay or fall back; moving closer is not allowed.
    • Critical Failure: Lose 1 Blizzard Token and start at the same or farther back position.

  • Select Pursuit Level:

    • Closer = More encounters, fewer weather hazards.
    • Farther = Fewer encounters, more storm exposure.

2. Travel Phase

  • Roll for Events: Encounter, Hazard, and Weather. Handle each with group decisions (avoid, cautiously proceed, or engage).
  • Event Resolution: Successes may avoid penalties, while failures may cost Blizzard Tokens, resources, or impose exhaustion.

3. Evening Phase

  • Camp Setup Challenge: DC 14 (Strength, Dexterity, Survival, or Wisdom). Results affect recovery:

    • Full Success: Long rest, regain 1 Blizzard Token.
    • Partial Success: Short rest, risk of exhaustion.
    • Failure: Constitution save to avoid exhaustion, no Blizzard Token recovery.

  • Spend Blizzard Tokens: Gain advantages for the next day, such as skipping hazards or reducing exhaustion risk.

Blizzard Token Depletion States

Tokens Remaining Depletion State Effect
4 Tokens Strained Endurance +2 to all DCs (Weather, Hazard, Camp Setup/Breakdown).
2 Tokens Critical Reserves Daily Constitution saves (DC 14) to avoid exhaustion.
0 Tokens Severe Exposure Constitution saves (DC 16) to avoid exhaustion. No rest.

Losing the Horde

  • If forced back at the Far Shadow position and cannot spend Blizzard Tokens to prevent it, the horde escapes.
  • This doesn’t end the campaign but shifts the narrative, with consequences for failure.

Your Objective

  • Balance risk and reward between staying close to the horde for safety or venturing farther for fewer encounters.
  • Manage your Blizzard Tokens wisely, as they’re critical for survival.
  • Plan collaboratively and use your character’s skills to overcome the relentless storm!
